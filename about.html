Team Philosophy and Student Mission

  • Our goal is to achieve a great Big Ten University education combined with a fun and rewarding college hockey experience
  • We value a rich Big Ten University experience that balances academics, athletics and an exceptional on-campus college life
  • Players play for the love of the game, the team connection and for the competitive experience that the game of hockey provides
  • Student-run leadership and administration under the University of Minnesota Student Union and Sports Clubs Programs

Facilities

Ridder Arena

We are proud to say that Ridder Arena is our home for both practices and home games.

  • Ridder Arena was opened in 2002, and it was the first arena built specifically for Women's College Hockey.
  • The arena seats 3400 people. This capacity includes 200 club seats and 9 sky boxes. We are very proud to have this beautiful facility be our home.
  • Team locker room includes an equipment storage where players can keep their equipment during the season and off-season

University Recreation and Wellness Center

Athletes on our team have full access to the Recreation and Wellness facility located on campus. The Recreation and Wellness facility offers our team access to:

  • ​Expanded Weight / Fitness Areas: The main entry level now contains a terraced, four level, state-of-the-art cardiovascular and strength training area, allowing multiple zones to suit the individual needs of students.
  • Indoor Cycling Studio: A new tiered cycling studio offers space for 45 bikes and a cutting edge sound and video system.
  • Private area for non-invasive health screenings
  • Body Composition Testing
  • Wellness Information Area

About ACHA

  • The American Collegiate Hockey Association (ACHA) was founded in 1991 and is the National Governing Body for Non-NCAA Collegiate Hockey throughout the United States
  • This organization of more than 461 colleges and university-affiliated teams representing 48 states and 2 Canadian provinces provides a structure, regulates operations, and promotes the sport of Hockey nationwide
  • The primary mission of the ACHA is to support the growth of Collegiate Hockey while emphasizing academic performance, personal development and opportunities for regional, national and international competition

All above information is provided by the ACHA
